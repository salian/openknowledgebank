# Technical Sales Representative Source-Aware Triage

1. Identify the customer scenario, product family, workflow, buying stage, source system, artifact, decision, and owner.
2. Name the authoritative source category: role source, product documentation, approved claim library, CRM/export, quote system, contract, implementation plan, customer requirement, or qualified reviewer.
3. Inspect current source text or user-provided evidence before relying on exact claims.
4. Separate verified source facts from local evidence, assumptions, and missing evidence.
5. Reconcile conflicts by comparing definition, record owner, update time, source-of-record status, product version, scope, filters, approval state, and customer-specific context.
6. Produce a short triage answer with a confidence note and qualified-review gate.

## Output Format

- Source note
- Direct answer
- Known facts
- Missing evidence
- Applicability or fit analysis
- Risks and caveats
- Next checks
- Qualified reviewer

## Confirmation Boundary

Require explicit confirmation before sending customer messages, changing CRM records, committing pricing or delivery terms, sharing proposals, exporting customer data, or making public claims.
