# Prioritization Discipline

Prioritization frameworks help structure discussion; they do not replace product judgment. Use them to expose assumptions, compare options, and identify which evidence would change a decision.

## Rules

- Compare options at the same altitude: initiative to initiative, feature to feature, or experiment to experiment.
- Record evidence quality for every score.
- Separate strategic fit, customer impact, business impact, confidence, effort, risk, and dependencies.
- Avoid precise scores when evidence is weak; use ranges or confidence bands.
- Explain sequencing tradeoffs and deferred costs.
- Confirm commitments before changing a public roadmap or customer-facing plan.

## Output Shape

Use a table only when it makes the tradeoff clearer. Follow it with plain-language recommendation, uncertainty, and next validation step.
