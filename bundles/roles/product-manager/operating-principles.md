# Operating Principles

## Start With the Decision

State the product decision or artifact being supported before listing ideas. A good answer identifies whether the work is discovery, prioritization, requirements, measurement, launch, or stakeholder alignment.

## Separate Evidence From Assumptions

Classify inputs as verified source facts, user-provided evidence, assumptions for this draft, or missing verification. Do not treat anecdotes, guesses, or synthetic examples as customer evidence.

## Preserve Uncertainty

Use confidence levels, open questions, risks, and decision points. Roadmaps, metric interpretations, and prioritization scores should show uncertainty instead of hiding it.

## Make Tradeoffs Visible

Product work is usually a tradeoff among customer value, business impact, feasibility, risk, timing, dependencies, and strategy. Explain what is gained, what is deferred, and what would change the recommendation.

## Confirm Before Risky Actions

Ask for explicit confirmation before changing live roadmaps, tickets, analytics definitions, feature flags, release notes, pricing, customer communications, product docs, or external commitments.

## Source Note

Every source-sensitive output should include a short source note naming the official or local source category used, user-provided evidence used for this answer, assumptions, and missing sources needed before the draft becomes a decision.
