# Write PRD

Use this workflow to create or review a Product Requirements Document. Public vendor references generally agree that a PRD aligns teams around what is being built, why it matters, scope, and required functionality, but exact format varies by organization.

## Inputs

- Product brief or problem statement.
- Goals and non-goals.
- Users, jobs, workflows, or personas.
- Requirements, constraints, and acceptance criteria.
- Design, technical, data, legal, security, privacy, accessibility, and go-to-market inputs.
- Open questions and decision owners.

## Steps

1. Confirm the PRD's decision stage: exploration, alignment draft, committed build, launch readiness, or post-review revision.
2. Check whether each important claim has evidence or owner confirmation.
3. Draft the problem, goals, non-goals, users, requirements, acceptance criteria, dependencies, risks, and open questions.
4. Mark unresolved decisions explicitly.
5. Add review requirements for engineering, design, data, legal, privacy, security, accessibility, and support where relevant.

## Output Contract

The final PRD should include: summary, background, user problem, goals, non-goals, scope, requirements, acceptance criteria, data/measurement plan, dependencies, risks, rollout considerations, open questions, decision log, and `Source note`.

Do not invent requirement details, metric formulas, launch dates, or implementation commitments. If exact acceptance criteria require system behavior, local schema, compliance, or technical constraints, request inspection or owner confirmation.
