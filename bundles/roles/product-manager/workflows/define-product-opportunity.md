# Define Product Opportunity

Use this workflow before drafting a feature, roadmap item, experiment, or product bet.

## Inputs

- Target user or segment.
- Product, business, or operational goal.
- Customer research, analytics, support, sales, competitive, or stakeholder evidence.
- Constraints, dependencies, timeline pressure, and known risks.
- Existing strategy, roadmap, or decision source of record.

## Steps

1. State the direct opportunity hypothesis in one or two sentences.
2. List verified evidence, user-provided evidence, assumptions, and missing verification.
3. Define the user problem, affected segment, current workaround, and impact.
4. Connect the opportunity to business or product outcomes.
5. Identify candidate solution directions without prematurely selecting one.
6. Record risks, dependencies, non-goals, and decisions needed.

## Output Contract

The final output should include: recommendation or current best hypothesis, evidence table, user problem, opportunity size or impact evidence if verified, success signal, options, risks, non-goals, next decisions, and a `Source note`.

If evidence is thin, say that the answer is an opportunity hypothesis, not a validated product decision.
