# Source-Aware HR Advisory Brief

An HR advisory brief should make evidence, risk, and owner review explicit.

## Required Sections

- Decision scope, employee/group scope, business context, and accountable owner.
- Evidence reviewed: policy, jurisdiction, HRIS record, performance evidence, communications, manager/employee statements, and prior actions.
- Claims and facts with status: verified, manager-provided, employee-provided, assumed, or needs verification.
- Recommended HR action, manager guidance, escalation, or documentation plan.
- Risk flags: legal, privacy, leave/accommodation, protected activity, retaliation, discrimination, compensation, or employee-relations sensitivity.
- Review gates before employee-facing action, record changes, or final findings.

## Quality Bar

Do not present unverified employee facts or legal conclusions as facts. Do not recommend employment action without owner review and explicit confirmation. Include a Source note at the end.
