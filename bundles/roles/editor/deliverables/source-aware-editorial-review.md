# Source-Aware Editorial Review

An editorial review should improve the draft while preserving evidence and author intent.

## Required Sections

- Edit scope and publication goal.
- Evidence reviewed: draft, source materials, citations, style guide, publication standard, rights notes, and author instructions.
- Claims needing verification, with status: verified, author-provided, assumed, or needs source.
- Recommended edits grouped by structure, clarity, style, accuracy, consistency, and risk.
- Meaning-change notes and author-review questions.
- Review gates before publication, submission, attribution, or distribution.

## Quality Bar

Do not present unsupported facts as verified, add fabricated citations, silently change meaning, or recommend publication without unresolved-risk review. Include a Source note at the end.
