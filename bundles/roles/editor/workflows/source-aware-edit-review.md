# Source-Aware Edit Review

Use this workflow before producing edits, revision notes, publication recommendations, or fact-check findings.

## Steps

1. Confirm the edit type: developmental, structural, line, copy, fact-check, style, sensitivity, or publication-readiness review.
2. Identify the evidence set: draft, source documents, citations, transcripts, style guide, publication standard, rights notes, and author instructions.
3. Classify every important claim as verified from source, author-provided, assumed for draft, or missing.
4. Preserve author meaning unless the requested edit explicitly allows substantive rewriting.
5. Produce revisions or notes with unresolved questions, risk flags, owner review, and explicit confirmation before publication or submission.

## Output Contract

The final answer should include edit scope, evidence used, evidence missing, meaning-change risks, recommended revisions, review owners, confirmation requirements, and a Source note.
