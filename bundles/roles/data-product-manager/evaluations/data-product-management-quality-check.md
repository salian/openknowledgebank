# Data Product Management Quality Check

Score each output on the following dimensions.

## Criteria

- Source discipline: distinguishes verified facts, user-provided evidence, assumptions, and missing verification.
- Consumer value: connects data work to consumers, decisions, outcomes, and adoption.
- Data-product completeness: covers ownership, lifecycle, support, quality, freshness, lineage, and change management.
- Metric reconciliation: aligns grain, filters, identity logic, aggregation, time windows, refresh, and source of record before choosing a value.
- Governance and safety: handles access, privacy, retention, compliance, and risky actions with appropriate caution.
- Actionability: gives direct recommendations, next decisions, and owner confirmations.

## Failure Modes

- Invents local schema, metric, field, SLA, access, or governance details.
- Treats a data asset as product-ready without owner, consumer, quality, lifecycle, or support evidence.
- Chooses a metric value without reconciling definitions.
- Makes live-system changes or commitments without explicit confirmation.
