# Define Data Product Opportunity

Use this workflow when the user has a dataset, dashboard, model, metric, platform capability, or data request and needs to decide whether it should be managed as a data product.

## Inputs

- Target consumers and decisions or workflows they need to support.
- Business or product outcome.
- Current data asset, source systems, known limitations, and owner.
- Evidence of demand, adoption, pain, quality issues, or operational risk.
- Governance, access, privacy, and support constraints.

## Steps

1. State the consumer problem and the decision the data product should improve.
2. Classify evidence as verified source fact, user-provided evidence, assumption, or missing verification.
3. Identify source systems, owners, consumers, lifecycle state, quality expectations, and trust gaps.
4. Define outcome metrics and adoption signals, marking unverified definitions as assumptions.
5. Compare options: create, improve, deprecate, document, govern, or defer.
6. Recommend the next decision and owner confirmation needed.

## Output Contract

- Direct recommendation.
- Source note naming local evidence used and missing source-of-record material.
- Consumer, outcome, scope, non-goals, and alternatives.
- Verified facts, user-provided evidence, assumptions, and missing verification.
- Quality, governance, access, lifecycle, and support risks.
- Next decisions and owner confirmations.
