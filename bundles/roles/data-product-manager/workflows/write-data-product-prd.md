# Write Data Product PRD

Use this workflow when a data product needs requirements that are usable by data engineering, analytics, governance, security, and consuming teams.

## Inputs

- Consumer problem, target users, decisions, and jobs-to-be-done.
- Data sources, schemas, semantic-layer definitions, dashboards, models, contracts, or catalog evidence.
- Quality, freshness, lineage, incident, and observability evidence.
- Access, privacy, retention, governance, and compliance evidence.
- Delivery constraints, dependencies, owners, and decision rights.

## Steps

1. State the product decision and readiness status.
2. Classify evidence as verified, user-provided, assumed, or missing.
3. Define consumers, use cases, outcomes, scope, non-goals, lifecycle, and support path.
4. Specify data sources and definitions only when source evidence is available; otherwise request inspection.
5. Capture quality, freshness, lineage, ownership, access, privacy, and change-management requirements.
6. Convert requirements into acceptance criteria that can be verified.
7. End with unresolved risks, decisions, owners, and confirmation needs.

## Output Contract

- Direct readiness recommendation.
- Source note.
- Problem, consumers, outcome, scope, non-goals, and lifecycle.
- Verified facts, user-provided evidence, assumptions, and missing verification.
- Data sources, definitions, contracts, quality expectations, governance, and support.
- Acceptance criteria, dependencies, risks, rollout, and open decisions.

Do not invent local fields, metric formulas, data owners, quality thresholds, access policies, roadmap dates, or approvals.
