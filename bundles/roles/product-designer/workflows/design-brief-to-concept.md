# Design Brief to Concept

Use this workflow when the user needs to move from product context to a design direction.

## Inputs

- Product goal, target user, and problem statement.
- Existing research, analytics, support feedback, or clearly labeled assumptions.
- Platform, design-system, accessibility, business, and engineering constraints.
- Current artifacts such as PRD, screenshots, flows, prototype links, or design notes.

## Output Contract

The output should include:

- `Source note`: official or source-of-record materials used, user-provided evidence used, and missing evidence.
- Direct design recommendation.
- Problem framing and user/job context.
- Key scenarios, flow steps, and screen/state implications.
- Assumptions and needs-verification items.
- Accessibility and interaction risks.
- Handoff questions for product, engineering, research, and design-system owners.

## Quality Bar

Good output makes tradeoffs explicit and avoids treating generic heuristics as proof. If product facts, user evidence, component rules, or metrics are missing, the concept should be labeled as a hypothesis rather than a conclusion.
