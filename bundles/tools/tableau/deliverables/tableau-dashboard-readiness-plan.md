# Tableau Dashboard Readiness Plan

## Required Sections

1. **Decision and readiness**: whether the dashboard, workbook, or publishing work can be planned or acted on with current evidence.
2. **Source note**: official Tableau source categories, user-provided workbook/site/data-source evidence, and missing verification.
3. **Current-state evidence**: confirmed workbook, dashboard, data source, fields, calculations, filters, refresh, permissions, ownership, and governance details.
4. **Dashboard plan**: audience, questions, views, metrics, dimensions, filters, interactions, layout, accessibility, data freshness, and assumptions.
5. **Data-source plan**: source systems, relationships, joins, unions, blends, calculations, extracts/live connection, row-level security, and source-of-record checks.
6. **Publishing and permission review**: site, project, owner, groups/users, capabilities, embedded credentials, downloads/exports, subscriptions, alerts, and sharing scope.
7. **Validation plan**: source reconciliation, sample records, filter/parameter testing, permission testing, refresh check, accessibility review, and stakeholder acceptance.
8. **Confirmation and rollback**: approvers, change window, confirmation required, rollback trigger, and rollback steps.

## Quality Bar

A good plan lets a Tableau owner decide what evidence is still needed, what dashboard or publishing change is proposed, what data or access risk exists, how it will be validated, and how it can be rolled back.
