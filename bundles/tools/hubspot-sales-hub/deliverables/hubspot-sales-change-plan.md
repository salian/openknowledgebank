# HubSpot Sales Hub Change Plan

## Required Sections

1. **Decision and readiness**: whether the change can be planned or acted on with current evidence.
2. **Source note**: official HubSpot source categories, user-provided portal evidence, and missing verification.
3. **Current-state evidence**: confirmed portal setup, object schema, properties, associations, pipelines, stages, reports, workflows, sequences, permissions, seats, API/integration context, or policy details.
4. **Proposed change**: target behavior, records affected, users affected, data affected, communication affected, and assumptions.
5. **Risk review**: customer impact, accidental outreach, data exposure, automation conflicts, integration impact, reporting impact, permission impact, consent/deliverability, and compliance impact.
6. **Validation plan**: sandbox, test record, limited rollout, report reconciliation, permission check, suppression check, acceptance criteria, and monitoring.
7. **Confirmation and rollback**: approvers, change window, confirmation required, rollback trigger, and rollback steps.

## Quality Bar

A good plan lets a HubSpot owner decide what evidence is still needed, what change is proposed, what could break, how sales or customer communications are protected, how reporting will be reconciled, how it will be tested, and how it can be rolled back.
