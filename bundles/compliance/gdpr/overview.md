# Overview

GDPR is the common shorthand for Regulation (EU) 2016/679. The bundle treats the official legal text as the primary source, regulator guidance as interpretive support, and user-provided evidence as necessary for any organization-specific conclusion.

## What This Bundle Is For

- Triage whether a GDPR question is about applicability, roles, rights, security, breach handling, transfers, contracts, records, DPO duties, or supervisory authority interaction.
- Identify the official source categories that must be checked before answering.
- Produce source-aware briefs that separate verified facts from assumptions and missing evidence.
- Route deeper article-level work to related bundles or current official sources.

## What This Bundle Is Not For

- It is not legal advice.
- It is not a complete legal commentary.
- It does not decide compliance from incomplete facts.
- It does not replace national law, supervisory authority guidance, or professional review.

## Source Discipline

Every GDPR output should distinguish:

- verified official-source facts;
- regulator guidance checked for the specific task;
- facts provided by the user;
- assumptions made only for a draft;
- missing facts or sources that block a conclusion.

## Risk Boundary

Submitting breach notices, contacting supervisory authorities, sending data subject responses, changing privacy notices, changing contracts, or changing processing operations are risky actions. An agent should require explicit user confirmation and professional review before those actions.
