# Overview

CAN-SPAM is the common shorthand for U.S. federal commercial email rules. This bundle treats FTC business guidance as the primary public guidance source, current eCFR 16 CFR Part 316 as the regulatory source for the CAN-SPAM Rule, current U.S. Code or GovInfo text as the statutory source, and user-provided campaign evidence as necessary for any scenario-specific conclusion.

## What This Bundle Is For

- Triage whether a question involves commercial email, transactional or relationship content, sender identity, subject lines, advertisement identification, physical postal address, opt-out mechanisms, suppression handling, third-party senders, or sexually oriented material labeling.
- Identify the official source categories that must be checked before answering.
- Produce source-aware briefs that separate verified facts from assumptions and missing evidence.
- Route deeper legal, platform, TCPA, state, sector, or international issues to current sources or companion reviews.

## What This Bundle Is Not For

- It is not legal advice.
- It is not a full legal commentary or enforcement-risk analysis.
- It does not decide campaign compliance from incomplete evidence.
- It does not authorize sending cold email, using purchased lists, bypassing filters, or ignoring opt-outs.

## Source Discipline

Every CAN-SPAM output should distinguish:

- verified official-source facts;
- facts provided by the user;
- assumptions made only for a draft;
- missing facts or sources that block a conclusion;
- adjacent legal or platform questions outside this bundle.

## Risk Boundary

Sending email, changing campaign copy or subject lines, uploading lists, exporting contacts, modifying suppression or unsubscribe behavior, changing ESP automations, contacting regulators, or sending legal communications are risky actions. An agent should require explicit user confirmation and professional review before those actions.
