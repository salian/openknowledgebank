# WCAG Source-Awareness Check

Score each criterion 0, 1, or 2.

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Source note | No source basis | Mentions WCAG generically | Names W3C/WAI source category, target version/level, user evidence, and missing source |
| Conformance target and scope | No target or scope | Partial target or scope | Names version, level, content/product scope, method, and date/release assumptions |
| Evidence separation | Blurs source, product facts, scanner output, and assumptions | Some separation | Clearly separates verified source facts, user facts, scanner/audit evidence, assumptions, and missing evidence |
| Conformance/legal caution | Gives unsupported conformance or legal conclusion | Adds caveat after conclusion | Frames conclusion as verified, provisional, or blocked based on evidence |
| Professional review | No review caveat | Generic caveat | Specific review requirement for public conformance, procurement, regulatory, contractual, or litigation reliance |
| Next steps | Vague | Some useful steps | Concrete source inspection, evidence collection, manual testing, review, and decision steps |

## Passing Standard

For publication-quality output, the answer should score at least 10 out of 12 and must not score 0 on source note, evidence separation, or conformance/legal caution.
