# SOX Overview

SOX is a U.S. public-company compliance area touching financial reporting, disclosure controls, internal control over financial reporting, executive certifications, audit committees, and auditor oversight. This hub focuses on source-aware triage for the common agent tasks around Section 302, Section 404, and Section 906.

## Use This Bundle For

- Classifying a user question as certification, disclosure-controls, ICFR management assessment, auditor-attestation, or broader SOX governance context.
- Identifying which official source category should be inspected before answering.
- Producing a brief that separates verified official-source facts, user-provided issuer facts, assumptions, and missing evidence.
- Preparing a handoff for legal, accounting, audit, disclosure-control, securities, or CPA review.

## Do Not Use This Bundle To

- Decide that an issuer is ready to certify a filing.
- Conclude that ICFR is effective or ineffective.
- Classify a deficiency as a control deficiency, significant deficiency, or material weakness without evidence and professional review.
- Determine filer status, exemptions, deadlines, or exact filing obligations without current source inspection.
- Draft final certification language or auditor opinion language.

## Source-Aware Framing

When exact facts matter, inspect the relevant source category:

- SOX statutory text for section identification.
- SEC rules, releases, forms, and C&DIs for issuer reporting, controls, certification, management-report, and filing questions.
- PCAOB standards for auditor integrated-audit and ICFR audit context.
- Issuer filings, control evidence, deficiency logs, disclosure committee records, auditor requests, and governance records supplied by the user for company-specific application.

## Safety Boundary

Outputs are drafting and triage aids. Reliance for filings, certifications, control conclusions, auditor communications, regulator communications, board materials, remediation decisions, or live system changes requires qualified professional review and explicit user confirmation.
