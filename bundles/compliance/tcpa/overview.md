# Overview

TCPA is the common shorthand for U.S. federal rules governing many unwanted calls, texts, robocalls, robotexts, and fax advertisements. This bundle treats FCC TCPA guidance and current FCC rules as primary public TCPA source categories, FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule and National Do Not Call sources as related telemarketing source categories, and user-provided outreach evidence as necessary for any scenario-specific conclusion.

## What This Bundle Is For

- Triage whether a question involves live telemarketing calls, prerecorded or artificial voice calls, autodialed calls, text messages, fax advertisements, consent, revocation, do-not-call screening, caller identity, vendor responsibility, or exemption claims.
- Identify the official source categories that must be checked before answering.
- Produce source-aware briefs that separate verified facts from assumptions and missing evidence.
- Route deeper legal, state mini-TCPA, sector, privacy, platform, carrier, contractual, or international issues to current sources or companion reviews.

## What This Bundle Is Not For

- It is not legal advice.
- It is not a full legal commentary, case-law analysis, damages model, or enforcement-risk analysis.
- It does not decide campaign compliance from incomplete consent, revocation, DNC, vendor, platform, or message evidence.
- It does not authorize cold calling, bulk texting, fax blasting, purchased-list use, spoofing, consent laundering, or ignoring opt-outs.

## Source Discipline

Every TCPA output should distinguish:

- verified official-source facts;
- facts provided by the user;
- assumptions made only for a draft;
- missing facts or sources that block a conclusion;
- adjacent legal, state, sector, platform, carrier, privacy, or contract questions outside this bundle.

## Risk Boundary

Placing calls, sending texts, sending faxes, uploading lists, exporting contacts, changing consent capture, changing DNC or suppression logic, contacting regulators, or sending legal communications are risky actions. An agent should require explicit user confirmation and professional review before those actions.
