# Overview

OSHA is the U.S. federal workplace safety and health regulator within the Department of Labor. This bundle treats OSHA and eCFR sources as the public authority categories for federal OSHA questions, while requiring state-plan source inspection and user-provided workplace evidence for situation-specific conclusions.

## What This Bundle Is For

- Triage whether an OSHA question is about employer responsibilities, worker rights, standards, recordkeeping/reporting, posters, state plans, inspections/citations, or a hazard-specific source family.
- Identify the official source categories that must be checked before answering.
- Produce source-aware briefs that separate verified facts from assumptions and missing evidence.
- Route deeper work to related OSHA obligation bundles or current official sources.

## What This Bundle Is Not For

- It is not legal advice.
- It is not safety engineering, industrial hygiene, medical, or incident-response advice.
- It does not decide compliance, recordability, reportability, citation exposure, or required controls from incomplete facts.
- It does not replace state-plan analysis, competent-person review, workplace inspection, legal review, union/contract review, workers' compensation analysis, or professional safety review.

## Source Discipline

Every OSHA output should distinguish:

- verified official-source facts;
- OSHA, DOL, eCFR, Federal Register, or state-plan source categories checked for the specific task;
- workplace or incident facts provided by the user;
- assumptions made only for a draft;
- missing facts or sources that block a conclusion.

## Risk Boundary

Submitting OSHA reports, contacting OSHA or a state-plan authority, changing workplace controls, changing lockout/tagout or machine guarding practices, changing PPE or training, exporting incident records, or modifying live systems are risky actions. Require explicit user confirmation and qualified professional review before those actions.
