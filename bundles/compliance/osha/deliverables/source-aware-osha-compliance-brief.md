# Source-Aware OSHA Compliance Brief

## Direct Answer

State the likely OSHA issue and whether the answer is verified, provisional, or blocked by missing evidence.

## Source Note

Name:

- official OSHA, DOL, eCFR, Federal Register, or state-plan source category inspected or still needed;
- current source inspected or still needed;
- user-provided workplace, hazard, incident, policy, training, control, inspection, citation, or other evidence used;
- missing sources or facts that prevent reliance.

## Source-Family Classification

Classify the issue as federal OSHA, state plan, General Duty Clause, recordkeeping/reporting, Part 1910 general industry, Part 1926 construction, poster, HazCom, enforcement/citation, or other hazard-specific source family. If more than one applies, state the dependency between them.

## Evidence Matrix

| Claim | Status | Source or Evidence | Caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| OSHA rule, standard, or source category | verified / needs verification | Official source or missing source | Include citations only if inspected. |
| Workplace or incident fact | provided / missing | User evidence | Do not infer facts. |
| Draft assumption | assumed | Stated assumption | Do not treat as conclusion. |
| Risk or operational action | needs review | Professional review, explicit confirmation, or missing evidence | Do not take action without confirmation. |

## Analysis

Explain how the verified source category and user facts connect. If a conclusion depends on state-plan rules, employer size or industry, establishment facts, task/hazard details, medical-treatment facts, exposure data, training records, policies, inspection/citation documents, deadlines, or penalties that are missing, say so directly.

## Next Steps

- Inspect named official sources.
- Collect missing workplace evidence while minimizing personal, medical, and confidential data.
- Route legal, safety, industrial hygiene, HR, operations, incident-response, citation, or enforcement conclusions to qualified professional review.
- Require explicit confirmation before risky actions such as OSHA/state-plan reporting, regulator contact, control changes, policy changes, training changes, record exports, incident-record disclosure, or live-system modifications.
