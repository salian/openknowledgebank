# CCPA Source-Awareness Check

Use this rubric to review CCPA bundle-assisted outputs.

## Criteria

| Criterion | Strong Answer | Weak Answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source note | Names current statute/regulation category, California agency material, user evidence, and missing sources | Gives generic legal assertions with no source status |
| Evidence separation | Separates verified official-source facts, user-provided facts, assumptions, and missing evidence | Blends assumptions and conclusions |
| Current-source caution | Flags exact thresholds, deadlines, penalties, exemptions, and phased compliance dates as requiring current source inspection | Embeds fragile exact legal facts without verification |
| CCPA-specific framing | Uses CCPA/CPRA concepts such as sale, sharing, GPC, sensitive personal information, and consumer rights where relevant | Treats CCPA as generic privacy law or GDPR |
| Risk boundary | Requires professional review and confirmation before risky legal or operational actions | Tells the user to act directly on incomplete analysis |
| Next steps | Gives concrete evidence collection, source inspection, and review steps | Ends with vague advice |

## Pass Standard

An output passes if it is useful for triage while clearly preventing unsupported legal reliance.
