# Source-Aware CCPA Compliance Brief

Use this deliverable when the user needs a concise CCPA analysis for review.

## Required Sections

### Direct Answer

State the likely issue, the confidence level, and whether the answer is a triage view or a reliance-ready conclusion. Most outputs should be triage unless current official sources and user evidence have both been inspected.

### Source Note

Name:

- the official statute or regulation source category used;
- the California Attorney General or California Privacy Protection Agency source category checked;
- the user-provided evidence used for this specific answer;
- any missing source or fact still needed before the answer can be relied on.

### Evidence Table

| Claim | Status | Source or Evidence | Caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Official-source fact | Verified / needs verification | Current statute, regulation, or agency page | Include date or version when available |
| Organization fact | Provided / missing | User document, system export, data map, contract, or statement | Identify owner or freshness when known |
| Draft assumption | Assumed | Stated assumption | Do not treat as conclusion |

### Applicability and Obligation Analysis

Classify the issue. Explain whether it concerns coverage, notice, consumer rights, sale, sharing, sensitive personal information, GPC, contracts, data retention, ADMT, risk assessment, cybersecurity audit, enforcement, or another CCPA topic.

### Missing Evidence

List missing official sources, user documents, technical evidence, or business facts. If exact thresholds, deadlines, penalties, exemptions, or phased compliance dates matter, require current source inspection.

### Risk Boundary

State whether professional legal or privacy review is required before reliance. Require explicit user confirmation before any notice, regulator contact, consumer response, contract change, privacy notice publication, personal-information export, or live technical change.

### Next Steps

Give the smallest useful next actions: inspect source, collect evidence, verify implementation, prepare reviewer questions, or draft a review packet.

## Quality Bar

The brief should be useful to a privacy professional because it exposes source status and evidence gaps. It should not sound more certain than the inspected sources and user evidence support.
