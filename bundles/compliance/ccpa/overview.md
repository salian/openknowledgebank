# Overview

CCPA is the common shorthand for California's consumer privacy law, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act. The bundle treats current statutory text as the primary legal source, California Privacy Protection Agency regulations and rulemaking materials as implementation sources, California Attorney General pages as official public guidance, and user-provided evidence as necessary for any organization-specific conclusion.

## What This Bundle Is For

- Triage whether a CCPA question is about applicability, consumer rights, notices, deletion, correction, access, opt-out, sale, sharing, sensitive personal information, contracts, GPC, ADMT, risk assessments, cybersecurity audits, enforcement, or another California privacy issue.
- Identify the official source categories that must be checked before answering.
- Produce source-aware briefs that separate verified facts from assumptions and missing evidence.
- Route deeper section-level work to current official sources or future companion bundles.

## What This Bundle Is Not For

- It is not legal advice.
- It is not a complete legal commentary.
- It does not decide compliance from incomplete facts.
- It does not replace current California statute, CPPA regulations, Attorney General materials, user evidence, or professional review.

## Source Discipline

Every CCPA output should distinguish:

- verified official-source facts;
- current rulemaking or regulation status checked for the specific task;
- facts provided by the user;
- assumptions made only for a draft;
- missing facts or sources that block a conclusion.

## Risk Boundary

Submitting consumer-rights responses, contacting regulators, sending legal communications, changing privacy notices, changing contracts, changing opt-out or GPC behavior, exporting personal information, or modifying live data processing are risky actions. An agent should require explicit user confirmation and professional review before those actions.
