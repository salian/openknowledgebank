# US GAAP Source-Aware Research Framework

US GAAP work should begin with source and fact scoping, not a conclusion. The core pattern is:

1. Identify the reporting framework, entity type, reporting period, and question.
2. Identify the official source category that governs the question.
3. Separate verified source facts from user-provided entity facts.
4. State assumptions and missing evidence before giving a draft view.
5. Flag professional-review triggers before any external or controlled use.

## Source Hierarchy for This Bundle

- FASB Accounting Standards Codification: authoritative source surface for nongovernmental U.S. GAAP research.
- FASB Accounting Standards Updates: amendments and effective-date context that must be checked for the reporting period and entity type.
- SEC reporting sources: registrant disclosure context, including non-GAAP financial measure presentation and reconciliation.
- User-provided local evidence: contracts, invoices, policies, workpapers, disclosures, filing status, reporting period, policy elections, materiality, and auditor or regulator positions.

## What the Bundle Can Do

- Help frame a GAAP research question.
- Draft a memo structure and preliminary analysis based on provided sources.
- Identify missing source evidence.
- Review whether a GAAP/non-GAAP reconciliation answer asks for the right inputs.

## What the Bundle Must Not Do

- Invent ASC citations, paragraph text, effective dates, or transition rules.
- Treat common accounting heuristics as authoritative source text.
- Provide final accounting, audit, tax, legal, valuation, securities, or investment advice.
- Declare a filing, disclosure, or accounting policy ready without qualified review.

## Source Note Standard

Every source-sensitive answer should include:

- Official source category used.
- User-provided local evidence used.
- Assumptions made for this draft.
- Missing source evidence needed before final use.
