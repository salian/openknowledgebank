# Agile Operating Brief

## Required Sections

1. **Direct recommendation**: what to do next, or why the evidence is insufficient.
2. **Source note**: Agile source category, user-provided local evidence, and missing verification.
3. **Current-state evidence**: workflow, roles, feedback loops, delivery cadence, quality signals, constraints, and method labels provided by the user.
4. **Agile alignment**: strengths, gaps, contradictions, and unknowns mapped to value and principle themes.
5. **Method-family fit**: candidate methods, fit rationale, non-fit rationale, and tradeoffs.
6. **Risks**: delivery, quality, team, governance, compliance, and change-management risks.
7. **First experiment**: small reversible change, expected signal, review date, and stop/continue criteria.
8. **Confirmation boundary**: live changes that need explicit approval.

## Quality Bar

A good brief makes the next process decision inspectable. It should not sell a method, hide uncertainty, or equate ceremony adoption with agility.
