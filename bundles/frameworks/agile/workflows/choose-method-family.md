# Choose Method Family

## Steps

1. Define the decision: adopt, adjust, compare, or diagnose a method.
2. Inspect work type: product discovery, feature delivery, operational flow, platform work, regulated delivery, or scaled dependency management.
3. Inspect uncertainty and feedback needs.
4. Inspect flow: batch size, queues, blocked work, release cadence, and interrupt load.
5. Inspect quality needs: automation, design health, defect trends, deployment risk, and technical debt.
6. Inspect governance and scale: dependencies, portfolio planning, compliance, contracting, and coordination overhead.
7. Compare method families as candidates, not prescriptions.
8. Recommend the smallest useful experiment and the evidence needed to revisit the choice.

## Output

Use the recommendation and tradeoff sections in [Agile Operating Brief](../deliverables/agile-operating-brief.md).

## Do Not Claim

- Do not say a team needs Scrum, Kanban, XP, SAFe, or another method from team size alone.
- Do not treat velocity, story points, or tool-board status as universal success metrics.
- Do not prescribe process or staffing changes without confirmation.
