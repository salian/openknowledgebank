# Agile Principles

Use the Agile principles as a lens for delivery decisions, not as a slogan list. The public principles emphasize customer satisfaction through early and continuous valuable delivery, welcoming change, frequent working software, daily collaboration between business and development, motivated teams, rich communication, working software as progress, sustainable pace, technical excellence and good design, simplicity, emergent design from teams, and regular reflection with adjustment.

## Review Questions

- What value reaches users or customers early and repeatedly?
- What feedback changes the plan?
- What usable product evidence demonstrates progress?
- Where are business, product, design, engineering, QA, operations, and users collaborating directly?
- What engineering quality practices protect long-term adaptability?
- Which work is being simplified or intentionally not done?
- How does the team inspect and adapt its own behavior?

## Anti-Patterns

- Calling a fixed-scope delivery plan Agile because it is split into short intervals.
- Treating velocity as a universal productivity target.
- Adding ceremonies without improving feedback, quality, or decision speed.
- Using Agile language to hide unsustainable pace or unclear accountability.
- Recommending a scaling framework before diagnosing dependency, governance, and flow problems.
