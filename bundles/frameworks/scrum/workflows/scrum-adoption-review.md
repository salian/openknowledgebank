# Scrum Adoption Review

1. Capture the user's objective and current pain: predictability, quality, feedback, ownership, prioritization, stakeholder review, or team coordination.
2. Ask for local evidence of accountabilities, events, artifacts, commitments, workflow, and constraints.
3. Check whether the issue is about Scrum, a broader Agile operating model, a tool workflow, staffing, governance, or product strategy.
4. Classify local variations as evidenced practice, context-driven adaptation, unresolved gap, or risky change.
5. Recommend a small experiment or evidence-gathering step before changing the operating model.
6. List any live process or people-impacting changes that require confirmation.
