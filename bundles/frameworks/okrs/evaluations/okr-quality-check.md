# OKR Quality Check

Score each criterion from 0 to 3.

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Source discipline | Invents or omits sources | Mentions sources vaguely | Separates public framework and user evidence | Clearly names source categories, user evidence, assumptions, and missing verification |
| Objective quality | Task list or vague slogans | Some outcome language | Clear outcome but limited scope clarity | Clear outcome, owner/scope/time horizon, and strategic link |
| Key result measurability | No measurable evidence | Metrics are vague or unsupported | Metrics mostly measurable with some gaps | Metrics have definitions, baseline/target evidence, owner, and source of record |
| Local policy handling | Assumes cadence/scoring/tool process | Notes local process but still prescribes | Uses local policy when provided | Requires local policy for cadence, scoring, committed/aspirational labels, and tool fields |
| Risk and confirmation handling | Ignores live-system or employee-impact risks | Generic risk warning | Names relevant risks | Requires confirmation before live tool, HR, compensation, employee communication, or executive-commitment changes |

## Passing Bar

A publish-quality answer should score at least 11 of 15 and must score 2 or higher on source discipline and local policy handling.
