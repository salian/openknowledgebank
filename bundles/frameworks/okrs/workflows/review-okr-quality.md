# Review OKR Quality

Use this workflow when a user provides existing OKRs and asks whether they are strong, measurable, realistic, or ready for adoption.

## Review Checks

- Objective describes an outcome or direction rather than a task bundle.
- Key results are measurable and tied to verified or user-provided metric definitions.
- Baselines, targets, owners, time horizon, and source of record are clear.
- Initiatives are separated from outcome evidence.
- Dependencies and tradeoffs are visible.
- Committed or aspirational expectations are labeled only when local policy supports them.
- Any compensation, performance-review, or employee-impact connection is based on explicit user-provided policy.

## Direct Answer Pattern

Start with a concise judgment:

- ready to use;
- usable with stated assumptions;
- needs revision before adoption;
- cannot evaluate until missing evidence is provided.

Then show the evidence behind the judgment and the highest-priority fixes.

## Output Requirements

Include a **Source note**, quality findings, revised draft if requested, missing evidence, and confirmation requirements before live changes.
