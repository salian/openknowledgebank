# Draft OKRs

Use this workflow when a user asks for new or revised OKRs.

## Inputs

- Strategic priority or decision context.
- Team or owner boundary.
- Time horizon and cadence.
- Metric definitions, baselines, and targets.
- Local OKR policy, including scoring and committed/aspirational conventions if available.
- Known initiatives, dependencies, and constraints.

## Steps

1. Identify the outcome the objective should describe.
2. Ask for missing local evidence instead of inventing metrics, targets, owners, or cadence.
3. Draft each objective in plain language with a clear scope.
4. Draft key results as measurable evidence tied to verified or user-provided metric definitions.
5. Separate initiatives, projects, and experiments from key results.
6. Label committed or aspirational expectations only when the user's local process supports that label.
7. Add risks, dependencies, and metric-quality notes.
8. Produce an [OKR Operating Brief](../deliverables/okr-operating-brief.md).

## Output Requirements

The output must include:

- Source note.
- Draft OKRs.
- Evidence used.
- Assumptions.
- Missing verification.
- Confirmation needed before live system, HR, employee communication, or executive-commitment changes.
